# Bookstore Analytics

## Project Overview

This project analyzes business data from the Bookshop dataset and communicates insights through well-designed visualizations, dashboards, and storyboards that support data-driven decision-making. The project follows a **think-then-do approach**, where stakeholders, decisions, and visualization plans are clearly defined prior to implementation.

## Stakeholders & Storyboards

### 1. Sales Manager — Sales Performance Storyboard

**Key Questions:**
- What is the overall sales performance in terms of total sales, orders, and quantity sold?
- Are current sales levels meeting business expectations and revenue targets?
- How does sales performance vary over time — are there seasonal patterns?
- What is the average order value, and does it reflect effective pricing strategies?
- How is revenue distributed across orders?
- Which book series generate the highest sales revenue, and which underperform?
- Is there a relationship between customer ratings and sales performance?
- Which products or series should be prioritized for promotion or pricing adjustments?

---

### 2. Bookstore Owner / Inventory & Operations Manager — Bookstore Performance Storyboard

**Key Questions:**
- What is the overall financial health based on net revenue, units sold, and average selling price?
- Which books, authors, and genres contribute the most to total revenue?
- How is revenue distributed across genres, publishers, and authors?
- Are there specific books or genres that should be restocked more frequently or deprioritized?
- How do customer ratings relate to checkout trends and revenue?
- Which publishers and authors represent the strongest business partnerships?
- What are the key revenue drivers, and where do potential risks or inefficiencies exist?

---

### 3. Publisher — Publisher Performance & Strategy Storyboard

**Key Questions:**
- What is the total revenue generated, and how stable is it over time?
- Are revenue trends showing growth, decline, or volatility?
- Which genres contribute the most to publisher revenue?
- How do discounts impact revenue, and which discount strategies are most effective?
- Is revenue highly concentrated among a small number of top-performing titles?
- How does revenue performance vary across geographic regions?
- What is the publisher's market share relative to competitors?
- How should pricing, discounting, and release strategies be adjusted to maximize long-term revenue?

---

### 4. Author — Author Success & Performance Storyboards

*Storyboards: Author Checkouts and Comparisons, Author Success, Top Selling Titles*

**Key Questions:**
- How many times are an author's books being checked out or sold?
- How does marketing spend affect book checkouts and sales?
- How does monthly and seasonal performance impact overall book success?
- How do an author's ratings compare to peers?
- What characteristics (writing habits, awards, tours) are associated with top-performing authors?
- Which titles perform best, and what common traits do they share?
- Should discounts be applied to improve sales performance?

---

### 5. Customer — Customer-Centric Purchasing Journey Storyboard

**Key Questions:**
- How do book prices vary across formats (Hardcover, Paperback, Mass Market)?
- Which formats offer the best value for money considering price and perceived quality?
- How have book prices changed over time — is it better to buy now or wait?
- Is there a relationship between book popularity and price?
- Do higher-priced formats consistently receive higher ratings?
- Which formats and titles provide the best balance between affordability, popularity, and customer satisfaction?
- How can customers make more informed, value-driven purchasing decisions?
